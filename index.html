So.. why Horizons?

I had found out my interest in tech late in the game and by then, it was too late to switch majors. Even then, I talked to my CompSci friends and about the major and how they wish it was more practical. I love my school, and while theory is suuuper important, I wanted to BUILD things. I had been searching for a way to learn coding or programming in a practical way for a while now-- visited a bunch of bootcamps in SF, read through many reviews online, etc. And Horizons appealed to me most because:

A strong and young community. Community is extremely important to me and I think being in this environment of SUPER COOL and passionate kids will push me in unimaginable ways. Admire that it's one of the few programs I know that reels in a younger generation, not just the working population.

More than just coding. 80% of the time coding. 20% of the time meeting AMAZING leaders in the tech industry. There's a strong emphasis on creating the next generation of entrepreneurs, not just people who can write code. What a great investment on young people and entrepreneurs.

Emphasis on school, less a bootcamp. A big critique of coding bootcamps is that they only teach their students how to get a job. When I was interviewing, I was told that there were classes and lessons that emphasized algorithms and data structures, things I think are important to learn for software engineering. The format may be bootcamp-style, but the "school" part of was very much emphasized.

Greaaaaat timing. I'm interning in a Robotics company doing UX/UI Design, and working with amazing engineers. I love my job, but, despite being the HTML and CSS guru, I had a huge frustration of not understanding the more technical side of things. They taught me React and Redux and I implemented a feature and it was so fulfilling -- I wanted more! I saw an email about this and I immediately wanted in.
